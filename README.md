Learning Lab Clinical Communication Demo

A Streamlit prototype for interactive clinical communication training that lets learners shape live clinical encounters through short, high-impact intervention moves. The app is designed as a Learning Lab demo experience for in-person workshops and online exploration, with a focus on making communication choices more visible, discussable, and replayable.

This prototype currently features two scenario-based experiences: The De-escalation Dance, centered on behavioral health, escalation, and hidden medical triggers, and The Swiss Cheese Safety Catch, focused on patient safety, cumulative risk, and near-miss prevention. Learners can use Director Mode to steer a clinician during an unfolding visit, or switch to What You See vs What You Heard mode to compare literal wording with likely emotional impact.

The goal of the project is to explore how lightweight interactive tools can support communication training, reflective discussion, and facilitated group learning. It is intended as an early prototype for Learning Lab demos and future expansion into more advanced role-play, feedback, and scenario-based educational experiences.

Features
Streamlit-based interactive prototype
Director Mode for steering clinician communication
Split-screen reflection mode for visible subtext
Scenario-based clinical communication training
Facilitator-friendly layout for live demos
Learner state indicators for trust, clarity, and emotional intensity
Current Scenarios
The De-escalation Dance
Practice de-escalation communication in a tense Emergency Department encounter while identifying signs that the issue may be medical, not only behavioral.
The Swiss Cheese Safety Catch
Guide a clinician through a high-risk safety moment where several small risks may stack into a larger preventable error.
Purpose

This app was created as a prototype to test how role-play, structured communication moves, and visible reflection tools might support clinical education in a Learning Lab setting. It is not a finished product, but a flexible starting point for experimenting with workshop-based learning, scenario design, and AI-supported communication training.
